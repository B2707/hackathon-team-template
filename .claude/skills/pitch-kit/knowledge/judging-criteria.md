# Hackathon Judging Criteria

Standard judging dimensions used across major hackathons. Actual weights vary
per event — the Day-0 rubric extraction (docs/DAY0.md, Stage 1) overrides
everything here.

## Dimensions & Typical Weights

| Dimension | What judges assess | Typical weight |
|---|---|---|
| Technical Complexity & Implementation | Depth of engineering, working live demo, appropriate stack, novel use of sponsor APIs | 20–35% |
| Innovation & Originality | Meaningfully different solution, novel mechanism (not just a wrapper), uniqueness vs other teams | 15–30% |
| Impact & Potential | Size/severity of the problem, people affected, credible path to deployment | 15–25% |
| Design & User Experience | Comprehensible in 30 seconds, empathy for the target user, glitch-free demo | 10–20% |
| Pitch & Presentation | Clear problem, logical solution story, composure under questioning | 15–25% |
| Track / Sponsor Alignment | Uses the sponsor's tech/API/dataset, addresses the track theme, speaks the sponsor's language | Variable — often decides track winners |

## Common Judging Formats

| Format | Description |
|---|---|
| Expo Table | Judges rotate through team tables; 3–5 min per team |
| Stage Pitch | Teams pitch to panel; 2–5 min + Q&A |
| Video Only | Async judging from submitted video and description |
| Hybrid | Expo round narrows to finalists who then pitch on stage |

## Judge Composition Patterns

- **Technical judges:** prioritize working code and novel implementation.
- **Business judges:** prioritize market size and monetization path.
- **Domain experts:** prioritize accuracy and real-world fit.
- **Sponsor reps:** prioritize use of their technology.

Calibrate pitch emphasis to judge composition when known.
